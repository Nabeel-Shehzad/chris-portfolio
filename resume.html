CV Advice

Your professional summary is the first thing potential employers or collaborators will read. Make it count by summarizing who you are, what you do, and the unique value you bring.

NOTE:

Each job or collaboration is unique, so your CV should be too. Highlight the experiences and skills that are most relevant to the opportunity at hand. This personalized approach can make all the difference.

KEEP the conversation going! Don’t let a great connection fizzle out. Follow up with updates, relevant articles, or even just a quick “thank you” message to keep the relationship alive.

Conclusion:

Networking isn’t just about what others can do for you. Offer your expertise, share useful resources, or make introductions within your network. When you provide value first, people are more likely to help you in return.
