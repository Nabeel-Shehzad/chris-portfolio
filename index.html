My goal is to encourage and inspire prospective undergraduate students to pursue a degree in STEM with learning difficulties and ensure their voices are heard.

Can anyone pursue a degree in STEM?

Chris Sorkos used to struggle quite a lot with STEM subjects back in high school. During his teenage years, he really did doubt his abilities a lot. At first, he thought that STEM wasn’t meant for him until he was diagnosed with dyslexia which was a primary reason on why he was struggling to solve mathematical problems and took him longer compared to his peers. Motivated and encouraged by his teachers back in high school made him realise that he indeed had a strong passion for mathematics and physics after having finished high school as a valedictorian. Besides his learning difficulty, he realised that it operated as a strong internal motivation. Consequently, he wanted to be a good example for encouraging other students to continue their effort despite any kind of difficulties to pursue a degree in STEM.

Student Chris is a mathematician. More precisely, he is the Vice President of the mathematics society.
Ambitions I believe that everyone has the potential to succeed in STEM fields, and I want to help demystify these disciplines and make them more accessible. My ambition is to become a mentor and advocate, showing students that with perseverance and support, they can overcome academic challenges and excel in their studies. By sharing my journey and passion for science, I hope to inspire others to pursue degrees in STEM without fear, embracing the excitement and possibilities that these fields offer.
Research Even at this early stage of my studies, I investigate how massive objects like black holes can bend light, aiming to expand current knowledge from recent research studies. I specifically explore the effects of gravitational perturbations on light paths and the dynamics of spacetime at play, contributing to the broad field of cosmology.
Conclusion My goal is to break down these complex topics and spread awareness faced by those with Learning difficulties. Please do join on this journey of exploring the mysteries within the cosmos, while supporting academic inclusivity. I'll also be highlighting resources and strategies on how to apply for scholarships and potential undergraduate research career opportunities!
