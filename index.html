My goal is to encourage and inspire prospective undergraduate students to pursue a degree in STEM with learning difficulties and ensure their voices are heard.

Can anyone pursue a degree in STEM?

Chris Sorkos used to struggle quite a lot with STEM subjects back in high school. During his teenage years, he really did doubt his abilities a lot. At first, he thought that STEM wasn’t meant for him until he was diagnosed with dyslexia which was a primary reason on why he was struggling to solve mathematical problems and took him longer compared to his peers. Motivated and encouraged by his teachers back in high school made him realise that he indeed had a strong passion for mathematics and physics after having finished high school as a valedictorian. Besides his learning difficulty, he realised that it operated as a strong internal motivation. Consequently, he wanted to be a good example for encouraging other students to continue their effort despite any kind of difficulties to pursue a degree in STEM.

Can one strengthen one's mathematical confidence? Absolutely!

Eleonora Svanberg discovered early on that she had a knack for solving math problems and would spend time working out difficult numbers and formulas in her room. But during her teenage years, she began to doubt her abilities more and more. Was there a chance for a young girl to become a part of the somewhat male-dominated universe of mathematics and physics? She started posting videos on Instagram to express her doubts and quickly became a source of inspiration for others. Today, Eleonora provides motivation, inspiration, and, not least, information about math and physics to hundreds of thousands of followers via social media.

In 'Study with Me', Eleonora talks about her journey from a young math nerd to a doctoral student at one of the world's most prestigious universities. Math and physics are among the coolest things you can do – no matter who you are.

Advocate

Chris having read several scientific books in his spear time, realised that he had a particular interest in cosmology. For the past summer of 2024, he has started working on a handbook that he is going to publish later on in the year. Having had a high interest in theoretical physics, his research concerns the gravitational lensing of black holes with implications on cosmology and the Big Bang Theory, His research focuses on the discovery and characterization of gravitational lensing phenomena caused by black holes, the influence of these gravitational lenses on surrounding celestial objects, and the potential to study distant galaxies and cosmic structures.
